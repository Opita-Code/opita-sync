# Decision log

## Structural decisions

- OSF and Opita Sync are separate by design
- OSF remains the governed kernel and canonical authority
- Opita Sync remains the AI-first product/control plane

## Product decisions

- Opita Sync is not positioned as a universal engine
- the product is AI-first in experience and OSF-first in authority
- conversation does not directly execute
- governance stays in the corridor and does not become optional
- evidence, inspection and recovery are part of the value proposition

## Commercial decisions

- activation is tenant-first
- value starts when a tenant becomes operable
- commercial model is implementation / activation + usage afterward
- current strategy is controlled adoption, not broad expansion

## Domain decisions

- domain 1: `tenant configuration governance`
- domain 2: `tenant access and delegation governance`
- do not open a third vertical yet

## Experience decisions

- admin tenant, operator and approver are explicit roles
- tenant admin surfaces must remain usable without duplicating kernel truth
- operator and inspection/recovery surfaces remain core, not optional extras

## Framework decisions

- canonical contracts stay inside OSF
- policy and approvals stay inside OSF authority
- runtime and evidence stay inside OSF authority
- product surfaces derive from canonical records instead of redefining them
